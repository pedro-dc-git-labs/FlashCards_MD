FLASHCARD 29 — Modales en participio

Index

Verbos y formas verbales

Estructura: modal + have + participio · Uso: deducciones, crítica y posibilidad sobre el pasado

Fórmula base

Se usa modal + have + participio para interpretar o valorar una acción pasada desde el presente.

Estructura Ejemplo
must have + participio She must have forgotten.
might have + participio They might have missed the train.
should have + participio You should have called me.

Significado de los modales más usados

Estructura Significado Ejemplo
must have deducción casi segura He must have been tired.
may have / might have posibilidad en el pasado She may have left early.
could have posibilidad o alternativa no realizada We could have won the game.
should have crítica, arrepentimiento o consejo no seguido You should have studied more.
would have resultado hipotético I would have helped you.

Lectura rápida del significado

Deducción

must have + participio
Ejemplo: They must have arrived already.

Posibilidad

may / might / could have + participio
Ejemplo: He might have taken the wrong bus.

Crítica o arrepentimiento

should have + participio
Ejemplo: You should have told the truth.

La idea importante no es el tiempo verbal aislado, sino la interpretación actual que hacemos sobre un hecho pasado.

Formas afirmativa, negativa e interrogativa

Afirmativa

Sujeto + modal + have + participio
Ejemplo: She must have forgotten her phone.

Negativa

Sujeto + modal + not + have + participio
Ejemplo: She can't have forgotten her phone.

Interrogativa

Modal + sujeto + have + participio ... ?
Ejemplo: Could they have taken the wrong train?

Forma Estructura Ejemplo
Afirmativa must have + participio He must have fallen asleep.
Negativa can't / couldn't have + participio He can't have fallen asleep.
Interrogativa Could / Might + sujeto + have + participio? Could he have fallen asleep?

En negativo e interrogativo, la estructura sigue llevando have + participio. Lo que cambia es el modal y la posición de not o la inversión en la pregunta.

Errores comunes y corrección

1) Omitir "have"

❌ Incorrecto: She must forgotten her keys.
✅ Correcto: She must have forgotten her keys.
La estructura completa exige modal + have + participio.

2) Usar infinitivo en vez de participio

❌ Incorrecto: They might have go home.
✅ Correcto: They might have gone home.
Después de have, el verbo va en participio.

3) Confundir "should have" con obligación presente

❌ Incorrecto: You should have study now.
✅ Correcto: You should study now. / You should have studied yesterday.
Should have mira al pasado; should + base verb da consejo presente o futuro.

4) Traducir literalmente como pasado simple

❌ Incorrecto: He must have left = salió seguro, como hecho confirmado.
✅ Correcto: He must have left = deduzco que seguramente se fue.
Expresa inferencia, no necesariamente conocimiento directo del hecho.

5) Romper la estructura en negativo o pregunta

❌ Incorrecto: Can't she forgotten her phone? / She mustn't have forgot it.
✅ Correcto: Can't she have forgotten her phone? / She must not have forgotten it.
Incluso en negativo o interrogativo, se mantiene modal + have + participio.

Consejo

Practica cada idea en tres formatos: afirmativa, negativa e interrogativa. Así interiorizas que el núcleo siempre es modal + have + participio.
