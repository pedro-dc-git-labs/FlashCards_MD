Past Simple

Acciones terminadas en el pasado · did/didn’t en negativas y preguntas

worked /wɜːrkt/
Overview Table
PRONOUNS POSITIVE PRONUNCIACIÓN NEGATIVE PRONUNCIACIÓN QUESTION PRONUNCIACIÓN
I / you / we / they worked
/aɪ ˈwɜːrkt/
ai uórkt
 I did not work
/aɪ dɪd nɒt ˈwɜːrk/
ai did not uork
 Did I work?
/dɪd aɪ ˈwɜːrk/
did ai uork
he / she / it worked
/hiː ˈwɜːrkt/
ji uórkt
 he did not work
/hiː dɪd nɒt ˈwɜːrk/
ji did not uork
 Did he work?
/dɪd hiː ˈwɜːrk/
did ji uork
Regla: en preguntas y negativas se usa did, por eso el verbo vuelve a la forma base: did + work (no did worked).
-ed en “worked”: suena /t/ (no /id/), porque el verbo termina en sonido sordo /k/.

Datos principales

  • Acción terminada en el pasado.
  • Marcadores típicos: yesterday, last…, ago, fechas.
  • Auxiliar: did (negativas y preguntas).

Apunte fonético

  • worked = /wɜːrkt/ → termina en /t/.
  • didn’t suele reducir la vocal (schwa opcional): /ˈdɪd(ə)nt/.
Ejemplos

I worked yesterday.

/aɪ wɜːrkt ˈjestədeɪ/

ai uorkt yésterdei

He didn’t work last week.

/hiː ˈdɪd(ə)nt wɜːrk lɑːst wiːk/

ji dí dent uork last uík

Did they work on Sunday?

/dɪd ðeɪ wɜːrk ɒn ˈsʌndeɪ/

did dei uork on sándei
