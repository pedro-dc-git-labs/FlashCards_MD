Tema 8 - Verbos modales

Bloque 8. Verbos modales

Uso de modales para expresar habilidad, permiso, consejo, obligación, posibilidad y valoración del pasado.

Usage: Este bloque reúne modal + verbo base y la extensión modal + have + participio.

Idea base y estructura

ReglaExplicacionEjemplo
Modal + verbo baseEl verbo principal va en forma base.She can speak English.
Sin "to"Los modales no van seguidos de to.You should study.
Sin cambio por sujetoEl modal no cambia con he / she / it.He can swim. / They can swim.
Negativa con notLa negación se forma con modal + not.You must not park here.

Modales básicos y valor comunicativo

ModalFuncion principalEjemplo
canhabilidad o permisoI can drive.
couldcapacidad pasada o posibilidadWe could try again.
shouldconsejoYou should rest.
mustobligacion fuerteYou must wear a helmet.
have tonecesidad externaI have to work tomorrow.
may / mightposibilidadIt might rain later.

Negativas y contraste importante

FormaEjemploSignificado
can't / cannotHe cannot drive.incapacidad o prohibicion segun el contexto
mustn'tYou mustn't smoke here.prohibicion
don't have toYou don't have to come.no es necesario
shouldn'tYou shouldn't stay up late.consejo negativo
Contraste clave: mustn't no significa "no hace falta". Significa prohibido. Para ausencia de obligación usamos don't have to.

Extension: modales en participio

EstructuraUsoEjemplo
must have + participiodeduccion casi segura sobre el pasadoHe must have been tired.
may have / might have + participioposibilidad en el pasadoShe may have left early.
could have + participioposibilidad o alternativa no realizadaWe could have won the game.
should have + participiocritica, arrepentimiento o consejo no seguidoYou should have studied more.
would have + participioresultado hipoteticoI would have helped you.

Errores frecuentes

ErrorIncorrectoCorrecto
Añadir -s tras el modalShe can speaks English.She can speak English.
Usar to tras el modalYou should to study.You should study.
Omitir haveShe must forgotten her keys.She must have forgotten her keys.
Usar infinitivo en vez de participioThey might have go home.They might have gone home.
