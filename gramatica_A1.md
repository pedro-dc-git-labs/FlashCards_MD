## 1. Categorías gramaticales

Las categorías gramaticales permiten entender la función de cada palabra en la oración. En A1 son fundamentales los sustantivos, artículos, adjetivos, adverbios, pronombres, preposiciones, conjunciones e interjecciones.

### 1.1 Sustantivos

| Tipo | Uso | Ejemplos |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Comunes | Nombran clases generales | book, city, teacher |
| Propios | Nombran seres o lugares concretos | Sarah, Madrid, Monday |
| Contables | Se pueden contar | apple, chair, two books |
| Incontables | No se cuentan individualmente | water, music, information |

| Plural | Regla | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| -s | Regla general | book → books |
| -es | Final en -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o | bus → buses |
| -ies | Consonante + y | city → cities |
| -s | Vocal + y | toy → toys |
| -ves / irregular | Casos especiales | leaf → leaves; child → children |
| Invariables | Misma forma | sheep → sheep |

### 1.2 Artículos

| Artículo | Cuándo se usa | Ejemplos |
| --- | --- | --- |
| a | Delante de sonido consonántico | a book, a university |
| an | Delante de sonido vocálico | an apple, an hour |
| the | Cuando el referente es específico o ya conocido | the car, the water |
| Ø zero article | Generalidades, idiomas, meses, días, plurales generales | Water is essential. She speaks English. Cats are independent. |

### 1.3 Adjetivos

El adjetivo describe al sustantivo. En inglés no cambia por género ni por número y suele colocarse antes del nombre o después de verbos copulativos como be, seem, look o feel.

> **Cheat**
> En inglés el adjetivo no cambia por género ni por número.
> La posición más frecuente es antes del sustantivo: a small house.

| Aspecto | Regla | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Posición básica | Adjetivo + sustantivo | a red car |
| Tras verbo copulativo | be / seem / look / feel + adjetivo | The car is red |
| Invariable | No cambia en masculino, femenino, singular o plural | a red car / red cars |
| Comparativo corto | adjetivo + -er + than | taller than |
| Comparativo largo | more + adjetivo + than | more interesting than |
| Superlativo corto | the + adjetivo + -est | the tallest |
| Superlativo largo | the most + adjetivo | the most interesting |

| Orden habitual de varios adjetivos | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- |
| opinión + tamaño + edad + forma + color + origen + material + sustantivo | a beautiful small old round brown Spanish wooden table |

| Tipo de adjetivo | Qué expresa | Ejemplos | Observación |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Calificativo | Cualidad | big, nice, difficult | Describe al nombre |
| De color | Color | red, blue, green | Suele ir cerca del sustantivo |
| De tamaño | Dimensión | small, tall, long | Frecuente en descripciones |
| De edad | Edad o antigüedad | young, old, new | Antes de color y origen |
| De origen | Procedencia | Spanish, Italian, British | Se escribe con mayúscula si deriva de país |
| De material | Materia | wooden, plastic, metal | Suele ir al final del grupo |
| Demostrativo | Señala | this, that, these, those | Funciona con el nombre: this book |
| Posesivo | Indica posesión | my, your, his, her, its, our, their | Siempre delante del sustantivo |

### 1.4 Adverbios

El adverbio modifica verbos, adjetivos, otros adverbios o la oración completa. Puede indicar modo, tiempo, frecuencia, lugar o grado.

> **Usage**
> Los adverbios de frecuencia suelen ir antes del verbo principal: She often studies.
> Con el verbo be suelen ir después: He is always late.

| Tipo de adverbio | Pregunta | Ejemplos | Posición frecuente |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Modo | ¿Cómo? | slowly, well, fast | Tras el verbo o tras el objeto |
| Tiempo | ¿Cuándo? | today, yesterday, now, later | A menudo al final |
| Duración | ¿Durante cuánto tiempo? | for two days, since Monday | Normalmente al final |
| Frecuencia | ¿Con qué frecuencia? | always, often, never | Antes del verbo; después de be |
| Lugar | ¿Dónde? | here, there, outside, upstairs | Normalmente al final |
| Grado | ¿Cuánto? | very, quite, really, almost | Antes de adjetivo/adverbio o del verbo |

| Formación de adverbios de modo | Ejemplo | Nota |
| --- | --- | --- |
| adjetivo + -ly | quick → quickly | Regla general |
| -y → -ily | happy → happily | Cambia y por i |
| -ic → -ically | basic → basically | Añade -ally |
| Sin cambio | fast → fast, hard → hard, late → late | No existe fastly |

| Frecuencia | Adverbio | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 100 % | always | She always studies at night. |
| 80 % | usually | We usually walk to school. |
| 60 % | often | He often calls me. |
| 40 % | sometimes | I sometimes cook. |
| 20 % | rarely / seldom | They rarely eat out. |
| 0 % | never | She never drinks coffee. |

| Adverbios de grado | Función | Ejemplos |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Intensifican | Aumentan la intensidad | very, really, extremely |
| Suavizan | Reducen la intensidad | quite, fairly, slightly |
| Totalidad o suficiencia | Indican grado completo o suficiente | completely, enough, almost, just |

### 1.5 Pronombres

El pronombre sustituye a un sustantivo y evita repeticiones. En este nivel es importante distinguir su función: sujeto, objeto, posesión, referencia reflexiva, señalamiento, pregunta, cantidad no específica o relación.

> **Contrast**
> my = adjetivo posesivo: my book
> mine = pronombre posesivo: The book is mine

| Tipo | Formas | Uso principal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Personales sujeto | I, you, he, she, it, we, they | Realizan la acción |
| Personales objeto | me, you, him, her, it, us, them | Reciben la acción o siguen a preposición |
| Posesivos | mine, yours, his, hers, its*, ours, theirs | Sustituyen al nombre (*its apenas se usa como pronombre) |
| Adjetivos posesivos | my, your, his, her, its, our, their | Acompañan al sustantivo |
| Reflexivos | myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves | La acción recae sobre el sujeto |
| Demostrativos | this, that, these, those | Señalan personas o cosas |
| Interrogativos | who, what, which, whose | Forman preguntas |
| Indefinidos | someone, anyone, nobody, everything, many, few… | Referencia no específica |
| Relativos | who, which, that, whose, where | Introducen oraciones de relativo |

| Pronombre sujeto | Pronombre objeto | Adjetivo posesivo | Pronombre posesivo | Reflexivo |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I | me | my | mine | myself |
| you | you | your | yours | yourself / yourselves |
| he | him | his | his | himself |
| she | her | her | hers | herself |
| it | it | its | — | itself |
| we | us | our | ours | ourselves |
| they | them | their | theirs | themselves |

| Demostrativos | Singular / plural | Cercanía | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| this / these | singular / plural | cerca | this book, these shoes |
| that / those | singular / plural | lejos | that house, those cars |

| Interrogativo | Significado | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| who | quién | Who is your teacher? |
| what | qué / cuál en general | What is your name? |
| which | cuál entre opciones | Which bag is yours? |
| whose | de quién | Whose coat is this? |

| Indefinidos frecuentes | Valor | Ejemplos |
| --- | --- | --- |
| someone / somebody | persona no específica | Someone is at the door. |
| anyone / anybody | persona no específica en preguntas y negativas | Do you know anyone here? |
| no one / nobody | ausencia de persona | Nobody called. |
| something / anything / nothing | cosa no específica | I need something. |
| everything | totalidad | Everything is ready. |
| many / few | cantidad contable | many books, few chairs |
| much / little | cantidad incontable | much water, little time |

### 1.6 Otras categorías útiles

| Categoría | Idea básica | Ejemplos |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Preposiciones | Relacionan palabras | in, on, at, under, between |
| Conjunciones | Unen palabras o ideas | and, but, or, because |
| Interjecciones | Expresan reacción | oh!, wow!, hey! |

## 2. Verbos básicos esenciales

En A1 destacan to be, have got / have, do, go, come, want, like, need, can, live, work, study y eat. Estos verbos permiten presentarse, hablar de rutinas, posesión, ubicación, gustos y capacidad.

| Verbo | Uso | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| be | identidad, estado, edad, profesión, ubicación | I am tired. She is a doctor. |
| have / have got | posesión y relaciones | We have a car. He has got a sister. |
| do | acción general y auxiliar | I do my homework. Do you work? |
| go / come | movimiento | They go home. Come here. |
| like / love / hate | preferencia | I like music. |
| want / need | deseo y necesidad | She wants water. We need help. |
| can | capacidad y posibilidad simple | He can swim. |

## 3. Present Simple

El Present Simple expresa rutinas, hechos generales, hábitos y horarios. En tercera persona singular suele añadirse -s o -es al verbo.

> **Usage**
> Present Simple: rutinas, hechos, hábitos y horarios.
> Se forma con verbo base; en 3.ª persona singular se añade -s o -es.

| Tiempo | Cuándo se usa | Estructura afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Present Simple | rutinas, hechos, horarios | I/You/We/They work; He/She/It works | do/does not + work | Do/Does + sujeto + work? |

| Persona | Afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I | I work | I do not work | Do I work? |
| You | You work | You do not work | Do you work? |
| He / She / It | He works / She works / It works | He does not work / She does not work / It does not work | Does he work? / Does she work? / Does it work? |
| We | We work | We do not work | Do we work? |
| They | They work | They do not work | Do they work? |

| Cambio en 3.ª persona | Regla | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| -s | Regla general | work → works |
| -es | final en -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o | watch → watches |
| -ies | consonante + y | study → studies |

## 4. Present Continuous

El Present Continuous expresa acciones en progreso ahora, situaciones temporales y cambios en desarrollo. Se forma con am / is / are + verbo en -ing.

> **Usage**
> Describe una acción en progreso ahora o una situación temporal.
> Se forma con am / is / are + verbo en -ing.

| Tiempo | Cuándo se usa | Estructura afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Present Continuous | acción en progreso ahora o situación temporal | am/is/are + working | am/is/are not + working | Am/Is/Are + sujeto + working? |

| Persona | Afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I | I am working | I am not working | Am I working? |
| You | You are working | You are not working | Are you working? |
| He / She / It | He is working / She is working / It is working | He is not working / She is not working / It is not working | Is he working? / Is she working? / Is it working? |
| We | We are working | We are not working | Are we working? |
| They | They are working | They are not working | Are they working? |

| Formación de -ing | Regla | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Regla general | verbo + -ing | work → working |
| -e final | se elimina la -e | make → making |
| consonante final | a veces se duplica | sit → sitting |

## 5. Past Simple

El Past Simple expresa acciones terminadas en un momento concreto del pasado. Aparece con marcadores como yesterday, last week, in 2020, two days ago.

> **Cheat**
> En negativa y pregunta se usa did y el verbo vuelve a su forma base.
> Marcadores frecuentes: yesterday, last week, ago.

| Tipo de verbo | Afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Regular | worked | did not work | Did + sujeto + work? |
| Irregular | went / saw / had | did not go / see / have | Did + sujeto + go / see / have? |
| To be | was / were | was not / were not | Was / Were + sujeto...? |

| Uso | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- |
| Acción terminada | I visited London last year. |
| Secuencia narrativa | She opened the door, sat down and smiled. |
| Hábitos pasados sencillos | When I was a child, I played outside every day. |

## 6. Past Continuous

El Past Continuous expresa una acción en progreso en un momento del pasado o una acción larga interrumpida por otra más breve. Suele aparecer con when, while, at 7 p.m. yesterday o all morning.

> **Usage**
> Describe una acción en progreso en un momento del pasado.
> Se forma con was / were + verbo en -ing.

| Uso | Ejemplo | Matiz |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Acción en curso en un momento pasado | At 8 p.m. I was studying. | proceso en desarrollo |
| Acción larga interrumpida | I was walking home when it started to rain. | marco + interrupción |
| Dos acciones paralelas | While she was cooking, I was setting the table. | acciones simultáneas |

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Afirmativa | sujeto + was / were + verbo en -ing | She was working at 5. |
| Negativa | sujeto + was not / were not + verbo en -ing | She was not working at 5. |
| Pregunta | Was / Were + sujeto + verbo en -ing + ? | Was she working at 5? |

| Persona | Afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I | I was working | I was not working | Was I working? |
| You | You were working | You were not working | Were you working? |
| He / She / It | He was working / She was working / It was working | He was not working / She was not working / It was not working | Was he working? / Was she working? / Was it working? |
| We | We were working | We were not working | Were we working? |
| They | They were working | They were not working | Were they working? |

## 7. There is / There are

Esta estructura sirve para indicar existencia: hay uno, hay varios, no hay, ¿hay?

| Forma | Singular | Plural |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Afirmativa | There is a book on the table. | There are two books on the table. |
| Negativa | There is not a book on the table. | There are not two books on the table. |
| Pregunta | Is there a book on the table? | Are there two books on the table? |

| Expresión de cantidad | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- |
| some | There are some apples. |
| any | Are there any apples? / There aren’t any apples. |
| a lot of | There is a lot of water. |
| how much / how many | How many chairs are there? |

## 8. Verbos modales básicos

En A1 destacan can, must, should y have to en usos elementales.

| Modal | Valor | Estructura | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| can | capacidad / permiso simple | can + verbo base | She can swim. |
| must | obligación fuerte | must + verbo base | You must wear a helmet. |
| should | consejo | should + verbo base | You should rest. |
| have to | obligación externa | have to + verbo base | I have to study. |

| Forma negativa o contraste | Ejemplo | Idea |
| --- | --- | --- |
| can’t | He can’t drive. | incapacidad o prohibición según contexto |
| mustn’t | You mustn’t smoke here. | prohibición |
| don’t have to | You don’t have to come. | no es necesario |
| shouldn’t | You shouldn’t stay up late. | consejo negativo |

## 9. Imperativos

El imperativo da órdenes, instrucciones, consejos directos o invitaciones. Se usa la base verbal sin sujeto expreso.

| Tipo | Estructura | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Afirmativo | verbo base | Open the window. |
| Negativo | do not + verbo base | Don’t touch that. |
| Con let’s | let’s + verbo base | Let’s start. |
| Con please | please + imperativo | Please sit down. |

## 10. Gustos y preferencias con -ing

Después de verbos como like, love, enjoy, hate o prefer es frecuente usar el gerundio en -ing para hablar de gustos y actividades habituales.

| Verbo | Patrón | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| like | like + -ing | I like reading. |
| love | love + -ing | She loves dancing. |
| enjoy | enjoy + -ing | We enjoy travelling. |
| hate | hate + -ing | He hates waiting. |
| prefer | prefer + -ing / prefer + nombre | They prefer walking. |

Observación: en inglés el verbo que sigue a una preposición también suele ir en -ing: after eating, before going, without speaking.

## Anexo 1. Future Simple

Aunque no pertenece al bloque A1 principal, el Future Simple ayuda a ampliar el estudio básico. Se usa para decisiones espontáneas, predicciones, promesas y hechos futuros.

> **Usage**
> Future Simple: decisiones espontáneas, promesas, predicciones.
> Se forma con will + verbo base.

| Uso | Ejemplo | Matiz |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Decisión en el momento | I’ll open the door. | reacción inmediata |
| Predicción | It will rain tomorrow. | opinión o predicción |
| Promesa / ofrecimiento | I will help you. | voluntad |
| Hecho futuro | The exam will start at 10. | referencia futura |

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Afirmativa | sujeto + will + verbo base | She will work tomorrow. |
| Negativa | sujeto + will not / won’t + verbo base | She will not work tomorrow. |
| Pregunta | Will + sujeto + verbo base + ? | Will she work tomorrow? |

| Persona | Afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I | I will work | I will not work | Will I work? |
| You | You will work | You will not work | Will you work? |
| He / She / It | He will work / She will work / It will work | He will not work / She will not work / It will not work | Will he work? / Will she work? / Will it work? |
| We | We will work | We will not work | Will we work? |
| They | They will work | They will not work | Will they work? |

## Anexo 2. Future Continuous

El Future Continuous expresa una acción que estará en progreso en un momento concreto del futuro. También puede sonar más cortés al preguntar por planes.

> **Usage**
> Describe una acción en progreso en un momento futuro.
> Se forma con will + be + verbo en -ing.

| Uso | Ejemplo | Matiz |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Acción en curso en un momento futuro | At 8 p.m. I will be studying. | proceso en desarrollo |
| Plan previsto como parte de una agenda | Don’t worry, I will be picking you up at 8. | acción programada |
| Pregunta cortés sobre planes | Will you be joining us for dinner? | menos directo |

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Afirmativa | sujeto + will + be + verbo en -ing | She will be working tomorrow at 5. |
| Negativa | sujeto + will not + be + verbo en -ing | She will not be working tomorrow at 5. |
| Pregunta | Will + sujeto + be + verbo en -ing + ? | Will she be working tomorrow at 5? |

| Persona | Afirmativa | Negativa | Pregunta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I | I will be working | I will not be working | Will I be working? |
| You | You will be working | You will not be working | Will you be working? |
| He / She / It | He will be working / She will be working / It will be working | He will not be working / She will not be working / It will not be working | Will he be working? / Will she be working? / Will it be working? |
| We | We will be working | We will not be working | Will we be working? |
| They | They will be working | They will not be working | Will they be working? |

## Anexo 3. Fonética del inglés

La fonética estudia los sonidos reales del inglés. En este nivel conviene reconocer el alfabeto fonético, las vocales cortas y largas, los diptongos, el sonido schwa /ə/ y la sílaba tónica.

> **Usage**
> Lee primero la transcripción IPA/AFI y después repite la palabra en voz alta.
> En A1 conviene distinguir vocales cortas y largas porque cambian el significado.

| Elemento | Idea clave | Ejemplos útiles |
| --- | --- | --- |
| IPA / AFI | Representa sonidos, no letras | /iː/ see, /ɪ/ sit, /æ/ cat |
| Vocales cortas | Sonidos breves y tensos | ship, bed, cup |
| Vocales largas | Se alargan más y cambian significado | sheep, car, food |
| Diptongos | Dos sonidos en una sola sílaba | go, house, face |
| Schwa /ə/ | Vocal neutra en sílabas no acentuadas | about, teacher |
| Acento de palabra | Una sílaba suena con más fuerza | TAble, hoTEL |
| Ritmo y entonación | Alternancia de sílabas fuertes y débiles | preguntas y afirmaciones |

Consejo didáctico: al buscar una palabra en el diccionario, conviene mirar la transcripción y escuchar el audio antes de memorizarla.
